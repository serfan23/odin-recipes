Ghormeh Sabzi (Vegan)

Description

A classic, popular Persian recipe, Ghormeh Sabzi is originally an vegetable and meat stew, served alongside Basmati rice. This stew is considered a national symbol of Iranian cuisine, and was dearly beloved throughout all my childhood of having vegan dinners with my family.

While this stew classically has meat, it can be easily left out, and its ingredients of mostly herbs, lime, and beans are enough to fill you up regardless.

Without further ado, here's how you can make this celebrated stew at home!

Ingredients

    1 large yellow onion, minced 1 cup red kidney beans 1 cup: chopped cilantro, chopped parsley 1 bunch scallions, chopped 1 teaspoon turmeric 1 cup kidney beans, rinsed and drained 2 dried limes 2 tablespoons dried fenugreek Grapeseed oil, used to the required amount Salt, to the required amount 2 cups of water, or to the required amount

    Steps

      Soak 1 cup of dry kidney beans in water for 8 hours. Then, drain the excess water and cook the beans in a pot of water on medium to high heat. Bring to boil and then reduce simmer for 30 minutes, partially covered. Heat stove top to medium high heat and add grapeseed oil, then the chopped onions into a pot. Saute for 10-20 minutes before stirring in turmeric for 1-2 minutes. Add the rest of your herbs into a separate pan and saute for 20 minutes. Slice dried limes into halves and stir them into the pan. Add all the ingredients from the pan, fenugreek, the cooked beans, and salt into the pot. Add water to cover by 2 inches, bring to a boil over medium heat, and then cover completely to cook on a low heat for 30-45 minutes. Adjust water and seasoning to your liking. Enjoy!
